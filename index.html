Safeguarding for Young People in Care.

Safeguarding for young people in care is a critical responsibility that involves ensuring their safety, well-being, and development. This process includes protecting them from abuse, neglect, and exploitation, while also promoting their health and educational outcomes.

Keeping You Safe, Supported, and Heard.

Keeping You Safe, Supported, and Heard emphasizes the importance of creating an environment where individuals feel secure, valued, and listened to. This approach involves not only protecting people from harm but also ensuring they have access to the resources and support they need to thrive.

Empowering Voices, Building Trust.

Empowering Voices, Building Trust highlights the significance of fostering an environment where individuals feel confident to express themselves and trust that their concerns will be addressed. This approach involves creating safe spaces where people can share their thoughts and experiences without fear of judgment or retribution.

Introduction

Welcome to ArCare Guard, we understand the challenges that young people in care may face, and that’s why we’ve created this platform: to be a reliable source of information, guidance, and resources tailored to your needs. Whether you're looking for advice on how to stay safe, information on your rights, or access to support services, this site is here to empower you. We aim to be a trusted companion in your journey, helping you navigate any concerns and find the help you need when you need it.

Key Principles

Safety First

Your safety is our top priority. Every young person has the right to live in an environment that is free from danger, harm, and abuse.

Empowering You

You have the right to make your own choices and have a say in decisions that affect your life.

Protecting You

Adults should protect you from any kind of harm or neglect.

Respecting Your Privacy

Your personal information and feelings should be kept confidential and respected by those around you.

Providing Support

Adults should offer you emotional and practical support to help you navigate challenges and thrive.

Ensuring Access to Education

You have the right to a quality education that helps you grow, learn, and prepare for the future.

What to Look Out For

It’s important to pay attention to certain signs that something might not be right. If you notice sudden changes in how you feel or act, skipping school a lot, or pulling away from friends and activities, these could be signs that you need help. Recognizing these signs early can help make sure you get the support and care you deserve.

How to Speak Up and Report Concerns

If you ever feel unsafe or have concerns about your own safety or someone else’s, it’s really important to speak up. Start by keeping note of anything that makes you feel worried or uncomfortable. You can talk to your social worker, a trusted adult, or even reach out to the police if necessary. Remember, it’s always better to say something than stay quiet—every concern is taken seriously and can make a big difference in keeping you or someone else safe.
